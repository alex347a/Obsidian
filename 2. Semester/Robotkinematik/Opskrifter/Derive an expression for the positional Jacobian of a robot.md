$Jp(q)$ is given by the partial derivatives of x,y and z over the angles.
![[Pasted image 20250605171938.png]]
$$
\begin{bmatrix}
\frac{\partial p_{x}}{\partial q_{1}} & \frac{\partial x}{\partial q_{2}} & \frac{\partial x}{\partial q_{3}} \\ 
\frac{\partial p_{y}}{\partial q_{1}} & \frac{\partial y}{\partial q_{2}} & \frac{\partial y}{\partial q_{3}} \\ 
\frac{\partial p_{z}}{\partial q_{1}} & \frac{\partial z}{\partial q_{2}} & \frac{\partial z}{\partial q_{3}}
\end{bmatrix}
$$
#### example:
$$
\begin{bmatrix}
\frac{\partial p_{x}}{\theta_{1}} & \frac{\partial x}{\theta_{2}} & \frac{\partial x}{\theta_{3}}
\end{bmatrix}
$$
