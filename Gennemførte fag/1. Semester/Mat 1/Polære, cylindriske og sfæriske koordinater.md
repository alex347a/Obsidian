$$\text{To convert from Cartesian coordinates } (x, y, z) \text{ to polar coordinates } (r, \theta):$$
$$ r = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}, \quad \theta = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{y}{x}\right) $$

$$\text{To convert from Cartesian coordinates } (x, y, z) \text{ to cylindrical coordinates } (r, \theta, z):$$
$$ r = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}, \quad \theta = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{y}{x}\right), \quad z = z $$

$$\text{To convert from Cartesian coordinates } (x, y, z) \text{ to spherical coordinates } (r, \theta, \phi):$$
$$ r = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}, \quad \theta = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{y}{x}\right), \quad \phi = \cos^{-1}\left(\frac{z}{r}\right) $$
