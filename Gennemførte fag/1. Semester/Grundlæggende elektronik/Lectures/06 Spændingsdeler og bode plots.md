| Unity                           | A magnitude of unity (1) indicates that the output signal's amplitude is equal to the input signal's amplitude. In other words, there is no gain or loss in signal strength. <br>- When the magnitude is **greater than unity** (i.e., $∣H(jω)∣ > 1$  the corresponding decibel value is positive, indicating gain (amplification).<br>- When the magnitude is **less than unity** (i.e., $∣H(jω)∣< 1$ , the corresponding decibel value is negative, indicating loss (attenuation). Unity serves as a reference point: it helps distinguish between amplification and attenuation in the context of signal processing and control systems. |
